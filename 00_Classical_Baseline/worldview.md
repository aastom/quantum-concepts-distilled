# 00: The Classical Baseline

**Objective:** Solidify the Newtonian worldview that quantum physics fundamentally contradicts.

Before exploring the "weirdness" of quantum mechanics, we must have a firm grip on the classical picture of reality. The shock of the new is only meaningful when contrasted with the old.

## The Core Tenets of Classical Mechanics

1.  **Determinism**: The universe is like a clockwork machine. If you know the precise position and momentum of every particle at one moment (the initial state), you can, in principle, predict its entire future and past with absolute certainty. The guiding equation is Newton's `F=ma`. The future is not probabilistic; it is pre-determined.

2.  **Continuity**: Energy, position, momentum, and other physical properties are continuous variables. A ball rolling down a ramp can have *any* value of potential or kinetic energy. There are no "forbidden" gaps. Its speed can change smoothly from one value to the next without jumping.

3.  **Distinct Nature of Particles and Waves**: In the classical world, things are either particles or waves. There is no overlap.
    *   **Particles** are like tiny billiard balls. They are localized at a single point in space, have a definite mass, and when they collide, they bounce off each other.
    *   **Waves** are disturbances spread out through a medium, like ripples in water. They are characterized by wavelength and frequency. Waves exhibit interference (adding or cancelling each other out) and diffraction (bending around obstacles). They are not localized.

A single object cannot be both a particle and a wave at the same time.

This classical worldview is intuitive and aligns with our everyday experience. It forms the foundation against which all the strange and revolutionary concepts of quantum mechanics will be measured.
