# Key Differences: The Uncertainty Principle

This table summarizes the conflict between the classical and quantum views on the certainty of physical properties.

| Feature | Classical View | Quantum View |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nature of Uncertainty** | An engineering problem. Caused by limitations of measurement tools. | A fundamental principle of nature. Baked into the wave-like properties of reality. |
| **Simultaneous Knowledge** | In principle, we can know position and momentum to perfect, arbitrary accuracy at the same time. | Impossible. There is a fundamental trade-off. The more you know about position, the less you know about momentum (and vice-versa). |
| **Underlying State** | The particle *has* a definite position and momentum, even if we can't measure it perfectly. | The particle does not *have* a definite position and momentum simultaneously. The properties are inherently fuzzy. |
| **Formula** | No fundamental limit. | `Δx * Δp ≥ ħ/2` (The product of the uncertainties in position and momentum has a minimum value). |
