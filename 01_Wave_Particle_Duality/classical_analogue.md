# Classical Analogue: Particles vs. Waves

In the classical worldview, particles and waves are mutually exclusive categories. An object can be one or the other, but never both.

## Particles: The Billiard Ball Model

Think of a particle as a tiny, solid sphere like a billiard ball or a marble.

*   **Localized**: It exists at a single, definite location in space. If you shoot a marble at a wall with two holes, it goes through one hole or the other. It cannot go through both.
*   **Countable**: You can have one, two, three marbles, but not 2.5 marbles.
*   **Collision**: When two marbles collide, they bounce off each other.

## Waves: The Water Ripple Model

Think of a wave as a ripple spreading across the surface of a pond.

*   **Delocalized**: A wave is a disturbance that is spread out in space. It doesn't have a single, precise location.
*   **Continuous**: A wave's energy and amplitude are continuous variables.
*   **Interference**: If a water wave hits a barrier with two slits, the wave passes through *both* slits. The new wavelets created at each slit interfere with each other, creating a characteristic pattern of crests and troughs where they reinforce or cancel each other out.

This fundamental distinction is a core axiom of classical physics.
