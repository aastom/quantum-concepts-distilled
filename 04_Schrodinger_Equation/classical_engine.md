# Classical Engine: Newton's F=ma

In classical mechanics, the "engine" that governs how a system changes over time is Newton's Second Law of Motion: `F = ma` (Force equals mass times acceleration).

### Role of the Equation

Given the initial state of a particle (its position and momentum), this equation acts as a deterministic machine:

1.  **Input**: The forces acting on the particle (e.g., gravity, friction).
2.  **Engine**: `F = ma` calculates the resulting acceleration.
3.  **Output**: By integrating this acceleration over time, you can predict the particle's exact position and momentum at **any point in the future or past**.

The output is a precise, deterministic trajectory. There is no probability involved. The universe, in this view, unfolds like a perfect clockwork mechanism.
